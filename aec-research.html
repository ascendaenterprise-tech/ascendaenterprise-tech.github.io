NQ at Critical Confluence — What the June 14 Open Tells Us

The NASDAQ 100 futures opened Friday's session with an overnight gap that placed price directly into a weekly Fair Value Gap identified on the Sunday open. With the Fed's next meeting three weeks out and CPI data printing hotter than expected mid-week, the market is pricing in a "higher for longer" narrative that creates asymmetric opportunity in both directions.

Read full note →

The Setup

NQ opened Friday at 20,847 — precisely into a Fair Value Gap formed on the June 8-9 weekly candle. This level has acted as a magnet for institutional order flow three times over the past 30 days. The question is not whether this level is significant, but whether it acts as support or resistance from here.

Key Level: 20,800 — Major support. Below this, 20,650 becomes the next target. Above 20,950 confirms continuation toward 21,200.

The Macro Context

Thursday's CPI came in at 3.4% YoY — 0.1% above consensus. Markets initially sold off but recovered within two hours as traders interpreted the data as "not bad enough to derail the September cut thesis." This behavioral pattern — sell the news, buy the recovery — has been characteristic of this bull market cycle since Q4 2025.

The Fed Fund Futures currently price a 62% probability of a September cut. If that number drops below 50%, expect a more significant NQ selloff. Monitor closely.

AEC Trading Desk Bias: Cautiously long on pullbacks to 20,800-20,820. Stops below 20,750. Target: 21,100-21,200. Risk/reward: approximately 1:3.

What to Watch Next Week

Monday pre-market will be critical. Any gap up above 21,000 should be treated with skepticism — those tend to be faded in the current regime. Look for orderly price action with consolidation before making new highs. Aggressive gaps without consolidation are distribution patterns in disguise.

The Dollar, Gold, and Why Institutions Are Quietly Rotating

Something important is happening beneath the surface of a market that looks calm on the surface. The US Dollar Index has been quietly weakening for three consecutive weeks while Gold has held above $2,300 — a level that previously acted as resistance for over two years. This is not coincidence.

Read full note →

When the dollar weakens and gold holds, the typical interpretation is that institutional money is hedging against currency debasement. This happens during late-cycle dynamics — when the Fed is either cutting or signaling cuts, and inflation remains elevated enough to erode real purchasing power.

AEC is long Gold (GC1!) with a position opened at $2,318. Target: $2,420. Stop: $2,275. This is a macro hedge, not a short-term trade.

On the equity side, the rotation out of mega-cap tech and into financials, healthcare, and industrials that began in late May appears to be continuing. This is consistent with a late-cycle expansion where earnings breadth matters more than multiple expansion on a handful of AI names.

Atlanta Real Estate: Why We Are Still Buyers in a High Rate Environment

The conventional wisdom says you don't buy real estate when the 30-year mortgage rate is above 7%. At AEC's real estate division, we respectfully disagree — at least in specific submarkets of Atlanta where population growth, job creation, and inventory constraints create a fundamental demand floor that rate sensitivity cannot override.

Atlanta added 78,000 net new residents in 2025 — ranking third nationally behind Dallas and Phoenix. The south suburban corridor, particularly Stockbridge, McDonough, and Locust Grove, continues to absorb inventory faster than it is being built. Cash buyers represent 34% of transactions in these submarkets, removing rate sensitivity from a significant portion of demand.

OHOS (One House One Story Investments) is actively acquiring in Clayton, Henry, and Rockdale counties. Target properties: 3BR/2BA, 1,200-1,800 sqft, ARV $200,000-$320,000.

The thesis is simple: buy at a discount to distressed sellers who need liquidity now, renovate to market standard, sell or hold for rental income. In a rate environment that has frozen conventional buyers, cash buyers with speed and certainty win on price. That spread is where the returns are generated.

May 24, 2026 Futures

Why I Trade the NYSE Open — And What Most Traders Get Wrong About It

The first 90 minutes after the NYSE opens at 9:30 AM ET generates more volume than any other period in the trading day. For NQ and ES futures traders, the open is simultaneously the highest-opportunity and highest-risk window in the session. Here is how I approach it.

Read full note →

Most retail traders try to trade the first 5 minutes of the open. This is almost always a mistake. The opening range — roughly the first 15-30 minutes — is price discovery. Institutions are filling orders, rebalancing overnight positions, and reacting to pre-market news. Trying to trade in that chaos without significant capital and institutional-grade order flow data is guessing, not trading.

AEC Framework: Wait for the 9:45-10:00 AM consolidation. Identify the opening range high and low. Trade breakouts of that range with momentum and order flow confirmation. This alone eliminates 80% of losing setups.

The second critical window is 10:30-11:00 AM ET — after the initial volatility settles and trend direction becomes clearer. This is where the highest-probability continuation trades emerge. Price has typically swept liquidity on both sides of the opening range and is now committed to a direction. That direction, confirmed by order flow, is your trade.

The third window — which most traders miss entirely — is 3:30-4:00 PM ET. The closing auction creates significant institutional activity as funds rebalance end-of-day. These moves tend to be sharp, directional, and well-defined.
