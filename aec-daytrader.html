Step 01

Establish Bias

Z-score and standard deviation bands determine whether price is extended or at mean. This sets the directional bias before any trade is considered. If price is beyond 2 SD, counter-trend setups become viable.

Step 02

Mark the Structure

Identify BOS (Break of Structure) and CHoCH (Change of Character) on the 4H. These define the trend narrative — no trade is taken without knowing which side of structure you're on.

Step 03

Locate Confluence Zones

FVGs and order blocks within the bias direction are the high-probability entry zones. The indicator plots these as horizontal lines. Price returning to these levels is the signal — not a trigger.

Step 04

Execute with Order Flow

DOM reading and tape confirms institutional participation at the confluence level. SMT divergence between NQ and ES provides the final validation. Entry only when all three layers align — no exceptions.
